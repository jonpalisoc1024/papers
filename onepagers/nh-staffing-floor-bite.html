Where the staffing floor bites: Medicaid share, rurality, and the heterogeneous response.

The federal nursing-home staffing rule was vacated and repealed; the debate is back in the states. The average treatment effect hides the answer — the bite shows it.
Jonathan Palisoc Ph.D. Candidate, Health Services Org. & Policy
University of Michigan
The bottom line

Across 13 states that adopted numerical staffing floors 2018–2024, the binary average effect is contaminated by a post-COVID secular trend (a t−8q placebo returns the same number). The identifying heterogeneity all runs in the policy-expected direction: a monotone dose-response across bite quintiles, steepest among the most Medicaid-dependent and rural facilities. The bite × Medicaid-share × post triple interaction is +0.153 HPRD (p < 0.001).

03

The honest binary average.

The pooled CSA ATT is −0.143 — wrong-signed for a binding mandate. But a t−8q placebo returns −0.13: the binary average is contaminated by a post-COVID treated-state staffing erosion, not the mandate.

Bacon decomposition rules out forbidden comparisons (81% of weight is clean treated-vs-never-treated). Heterogeneity specifications survive because they use within-state-quarter variation a state-level placebo cannot replicate.

A

Why it matters now.

  • The 2024 CMS federal rule was vacated and repealed in late 2025; 25 states still operate their own floors.
  • Any future federal rule will land on the same heterogeneous facility distribution.
  • Medicaid-heavy and rural facilities are the binding cases; one-size-fits-all standards leave them with no margin.
B

What we did.

Built a 510,189-row facility-quarter panel from the CMS Payroll-Based Journal 2017Q1–2025Q4 (16,401 facilities).

Constructed each facility’s pre-policy bite; merged LTCFocus Medicaid-day shares and Care Compare rurality. Estimated Callaway–Sant’Anna ATT, dose-response across bite quintiles, and a TWFE triple interaction.

C

Why believe it.

  • Within-state-quarter identification can’t be replicated by the state-level placebo that breaks the binary average.
  • Resident-mix robustness: 7-control adjustment moves the binary point estimate from −0.145 to −0.139.
  • Rambachan–Roth HonestDiD bounds confirm direction.
→

So what?

Staffing floors work where they bind and where they matter. Any future federal mandate should pair the standard with technical assistance and rate-cell adjustments for Medicaid-heavy and rural facilities.
