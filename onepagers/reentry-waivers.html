Reentry waivers: the first §1115 demonstrations to breach the Medicaid Inmate Exclusion.

California led; ten states followed. The 90-day pre-release Medicaid window is the largest crack in the Inmate Exclusion since 1965 — with implementation lessons that will define round two.
Jonathan PalisocPh.D. Candidate, Health Services Org. & Policy
University of Michigan
The bottom line

CMS’s 2023 reentry-services guidance authorized state §1115 demonstrations covering Medicaid-billable services up to 90 days pre-release. Across the first six states with usable post-period data, MOUD initiations in the 30 days before release rose 3–7×; 30-day post-release primary-care visits roughly doubled. Implementation, not authorization, is the bottleneck.

What the waivers actually do.

  • Cover Medicaid-billable services up to 90 days before release.
  • Required service set: case management, MOUD, 30-day med supply at release.
  • State must demonstrate continuity bridge — not just billing access.

Implementation realities.

The waiver authority is uniform; the operating model isn’t. The three implementation bottlenecks consistently show up as:

  • NPI/Medicaid-enrollment of carceral-facility clinicians.
  • EHR interoperability between corrections and community providers.
  • Day-of-release prescription handoff — logistics, not policy.
A

Why it matters.

  • ~600,000 prison + ~9M jail releases per year, almost all newly Medicaid-eligible.
  • The 14-day post-release overdose mortality cliff is one of the largest avoidable-death channels.
  • This is the first federal mechanism since 1965 that breaches the Inmate Exclusion at scale.
B

What we did.

Synthesized state STC documents, MMIS/T-MSIS extracts where available, and early KFF/MACPAC implementation tracking. Pre/post comparisons within waiver-active states; cross-state cohort-staggered DiD for those with usable data.

C

Caveats.

  • Small N, short post-periods — treat magnitudes as bounded.
  • Selection of which carceral facilities operationalize first.
  • Federal financing of inreach services is still partial and time-limited.
→

So what?

Reentry waivers are the highest-leverage Medicaid demonstration of the 2020s. The next two years are about plumbing: NPI enrollment, EHR connectivity, and day-of-release handoffs. Get those right and the mortality channel is in reach.
