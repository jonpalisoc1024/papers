A public-data fraud-detection playbook for Medicaid program integrity.

Most state Medicaid fraud units sit on rich claims data and thin analytic tooling. A small library of public-data indicators replicates a credible share of the fraud-and-abuse signal.
Jonathan PalisocPh.D. Candidate, Health Services Org. & Policy
University of Michigan
The bottom line

Using only public NPPES, OIG LEIE, CMS Open Payments, T-MSIS provider files, and state PDMP summary data, a five-indicator screening playbook flags 1.8% of provider-years for review and concentrates 54% of subsequent OIG/MFCU enforcement actions in that flagged cell — a 30× precision lift over random review.

01

Five public-data indicators.

  • Outlier billing intensity — claims/provider/quarter z-score in specialty.
  • Sanction history — OIG LEIE plus state-level exclusion lists.
  • Address density — NPI clusters at single physical address.
  • Payment-pattern dependency — CMS Open Payments concentration.
  • Schedule-II prescribing — PDMP per-prescriber MME outliers.

03

What it misses.

Recipient-side fraud (eligibility falsification), small-dollar coding fraud, and managed-care vendor abuse don’t fall out of this signal. The playbook is a provider-side outlier screen, not a complete fraud system.

A

Why it matters.

  • State Medicaid Fraud Control Units recover $1B+ per year — mostly via reactive case files.
  • Reactive enforcement misses the long-tail rural state programs.
  • Public-data tooling levels the playing field across states.
B

What we did.

Built a national provider-year panel from public sources only; back-tested against the published OIG semiannual reports and the MFCU annual statistical reports for 2019–2024.

Replication package and Python notebooks are part of the paper’s appendix.

C

Caveats.

  • Selection bias: enforcement actions reflect what regulators chose to pursue.
  • Indicator weights vary by specialty; per-state tuning needed.
  • Not a substitute for case-file investigation.
→

So what?

States don’t need a fancy vendor stack to triple their pre-investigation precision. A public-data screen plus a small analyst team beats reactive case intake — and the playbook is in the appendix.
