A contaminated kink: when the ACA expansion breaks a workhorse Medicaid design.

A regression kink at Medicaid’s 50% federal-match floor has become a go-to identification strategy. Once you drop ACA-expansion state-years from the panel, the sign flips. That’s a warning, not a fix.
Jonathan Palisoc Ph.D. Candidate, Health Services Org. & Policy
University of Michigan
The bottom line

The FMAP regression-kink design recovers a positive aggregate Medicaid spending response on the pooled FY 2008–2023 panel — +1.49 — but dropping ACA-expansion state-years flips the sign to −2.53. An extended-window check (FY 2002–2023, +306 state-years) confirms the flip is structural, not sample-size driven. The non-expansion subsample is also observably unbalanced: 35% Southern vs. 13% nationally.

01

One design, two federal-match margins.

The §1905(b) FMAP formula has a sharp 50% floor. A growing RKD literature (Kumar 2020; Leung 2022; Giertz & Kumar 2024) uses that kink to identify how state Medicaid spending responds to federal generosity.

But on post-2014 panels, the formula isn’t the only federal-match margin: ACA expansion attaches a flat 90% match to a newly eligible population in expansion states only — and expansion choice correlates with the RKD running variable.

A

Why it matters.

  • The FMAP-kink RKD is now a workhorse for federal-Medicaid finance research.
  • Any paper using it on post-2014 data risks pooling two distinct match variations.
  • The non-expansion subsample is not a clean counterfactual — it’s a selected sample.
B

What we did.

Built a public-data panel of 816 state-years (FY 2008–2023) from MACPAC, HHS Federal Register, BEA SAINC1, and CMS/Mathematica LTSS reports. Replicated a sharp local-quadratic RKD with state-clustered SEs.

Pre-specified drops for ARRA, ACA expansion, and FFCRA §6008 enhanced-match episodes, then added an observable balance check on expansion vs. non-expansion state-years inside the bandwidth.

C

Why believe it.

  • Empirical FMAP slope at the kink is 0.88–1.04, recovering the mechanical 0.9487 from §1905(b) — the pipeline is correct.
  • Extended-window check on a 45% larger panel confirms the flip.
  • Caveat: the non-expansion estimate is itself selected — not a preferred estimand.
→

So what?

Future FMAP-kink analyses should pre-specify “drop all ACA-expansion state-years” as a primary robustness check, not a footnote. The headline aggregate response in this literature is not robust to that drop.
